What guests are doing

Arriving by invite. Moving through the collection at their own pace — touching, photographing, talking to the Zara Home team. Picking up a specialty cocktail or a passed bite. Generating content for their channels and editorial coverage for the press.

What the space needs to do

Be fully cleared before load-in so the collection owns the room. Have strong natural light for photography. Flow well for a cocktail-style reception — no bottlenecks, no awkward corridors. Photograph clean and editorial on phones and pro cameras alike. Signal "elevated home" without competing with the product.

Option 1 · Casa Ezra · Upper West Side · Historic townhouse · Available — all windows ✓

An Upper West Side townhouse.
Open kitchen, wisteria pergola, and four floors of light-filled residential character.

Why it works for Zara Home

Casa Ezra's residential character — warm materials, open plan, good natural light throughout — makes it one of the most natural fits for a home goods collection. The open kitchen with marble island becomes your reception counter. The wisteria pergola backyard gives a layered outdoor moment for photographers. The steel staircase reads architectural and editorial without trying too hard.

Three primary floors clear to open, walkable space. Easy to dress the rooms with the Zara Home fall pieces and photograph the collection in context without fighting the architecture.

Option 2 · Casa Luz · SoHo · Two-floor cast-iron loft · Available July + early Sept only

A classic SoHo loft on Howard Street.
15-foot ceilings, cast-iron columns, exposed brick, and oversized windows — two full floors.

The most architecturally dramatic space in the set — a two-floor SoHo loft on Howard Street with 15-foot ceilings, original cast-iron Corinthian columns, exposed brick, polished hardwood floors, and oversized arched windows on multiple exposures. The lower floor is approximately 3,500 sq ft of fully open column-framed space; the upper floor is a matched second level that books with it.

For a collection showcase, this space does the heavy lifting photographically before a single piece of furniture is placed. The column rhythm creates natural vignette zones. The multiple window exposures mean good light at almost any time of day. The cast-iron facade on Howard Street is the most recognizable fashion-event arrival in the city.

Option 4 · Sutton Tower PH77 · Sutton Place, Midtown East · 77th-floor penthouse · Availability TBD

A 77th-floor penthouse at Sutton Tower.
4,595 sq ft with direct elevator entry, corner great room, and views of the East River, Midtown, and Central Park.

Penthouse 77 · Sutton Tower · 430 East 58th Street · Interior 4,595 sf | Exterior 170 sf

Sutton Tower PH77 is a different register from the rest of the set: not a townhouse or a loft, but a brand-new full-floor luxury penthouse on the 77th floor at 430 East 58th Street. The scale is residential but the views — East River, Long Island Sound, New York Harbor, Midtown skyline, and Central Park — are unmatched in the portfolio.

The floor plan reads as a natural showcase circuit: the 33'9" × 17'11" corner great room with gas fireplace anchors the main collection display, the 18'3" × 15'3" corner dining room as a second showcase zone, and the 33-foot entrance gallery as the arrival and first impression. The direct elevator entry means guests step into the space rather than lobby-ing up.

For an elevated home goods collection — furniture, textiles, objects — a 77th-floor skyline backdrop is a meaningful editorial upgrade over a street-level space. It's a different ask logistically (loading, security, building coordination) but the result is unique in the market.
